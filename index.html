SpeechJudge: Towards Human-Level Judgment for Speech Naturalness

Abstract

Aligning large generative models with human feedback is a critical challenge. In speech synthesis, this is particularly pronounced due to the lack of a large-scale human preference dataset, which hinders the development of models that truly align with human perception. To address this, we introduce SpeechJudge, a comprehensive suite comprising a dataset, a benchmark, and a reward model centered on naturalnessâ€”one of the most fundamental subjective metrics for speech synthesis. First, we present SpeechJudge-Data, a large-scale corpus of 99k human preference pairs. The dataset is constructed using a diverse set of advanced text-to-speech (TTS) models across multiple languages and expressive styles, with annotations for both intelligibility and pairwise naturalness preference. From this, we establish SpeechJudge-Eval, a challenging benchmark for automated naturalness judgment. Our evaluation reveals that existing metrics and even state-of-the-art AudioLLMs struggle with this task; the best-performing model, Gemini-2.5-Flash, achieves only about 70% agreement with human preferences, highlighting a significant gap for improvement. To bridge this gap, we develop SpeechJudge-GRM, a generative reward model (GRM) based on Qwen2.5-Omni-7B. It is trained on SpeechJudge-Data via a two-stage post-training process: Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) with Chain-of-Thought rationales followed by Reinforcement Learning (RL) on challenging cases. SpeechJudge-GRM demonstrates superior performance, achieving 77.2% accuracy (and 79.4% after inference-time scaling @10) on our benchmark compared to a traditional Bradley-Terry reward model (72.7%). Furthermore, SpeechJudge-GRM can be employed as a reward function during the post-training of speech generation models to effectively enhance their naturalness and quality. We will release all resources from this study to facilitate future research in human-aligned speech synthesis.
